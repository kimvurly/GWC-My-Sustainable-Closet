About This Project

What is My Sustainable Closet?

My Sustainable Closet can be used as a web application and a mobile app that helps users practice sustainable and slow fashion, while also looking and feeling their best!

Fashion may not seem like it has a huge impact on the planet, but as explored in the "Impact" tab, it does! Therefore, anyone who loves to shop for clothes can also make a huge impact on the planet. Everyone wears clothes, and a lot of people wear fast fashion. My Sustainable Closet combats this by promoting slow fashion, upcycling, and the reuse of clothing.

A sustainable fashion finder...

The types of clothing that someone wears could enable others to treat them differently based on what social class their clothes reflect that they belong to. The reason why so many people buy fast fashion is because it's cheap and lets them express themselves without revealing their social class. Fast fashion creates clothing that all social classes can afford, getting rid of the ability to differentiate someone by their clothing.

Additionally, it's often hard to find brands that are sustainable but also sell trendy clothing. Fashion trends are quickly adopted through the practices of fast fashion, so it makes sense that sustainable brands do not often sell trendy clothing. Sustainable clothes are also often more expensive than fast fashion brands. Sustainable fashion brands are aimed towards those of higher social classes that can afford their clothing, which further divides people by the clothes that they wear.

To combat this problem, the main feature of my project makes it easy to shop sustainably for anyone who cannot afford traditional sustainable clothing brands, but also want to look their best and express themselves.

My Sustainable Closet combats this issue by introducing a feature that recommends to you clothes found from online thrifting websites or sustainable fashion brands that match your style! My project utilizes AI to achieve this. The user is led to a swiping screen where they are shown a piece of clothing and are asked to indicate whether or not they like it. The AI notes down what kind of styles and patterns the user likes and doesn't like in a database. The AI analyzes the dataset obtained from the action of the user swiping. The AI then goes through multiple databases from online thrifting companies and sustainable fashion brands that would agree to give my project access to its data. By analyzing these datasets, the AI matches the user's style to a couple of listings that would be fitting for the user to buy.

A virtual closet...

There are also a couple of issues that have arised from the growing trend of thrifting. Thrifting allows those who do not have enough money to afford stylish clothes to look and feel their best. However, thrifting has become more popular among the middle and upper social classes who can afford sustainable fashion brands, but are attracted by the low prices of thrifted clothing.

My project combats this by introducing a virtual closet feature. All of the clothes in a user's closet can be put into the system manually via search and links, but the user is also given the option to take a photo of their clothes and let the AI use image identification to find the piece of clothing online. This would be achieved by using a convolutional neural network, which is a machine learning model that can categorize images based on a large database.

The recommendations that the AI finds online can also be viewed in the virtual closet. This allows the user to play around with different outfits and helps the user decide if a piece of clothing is worth buying. This reduces overconsumption and makes online shopping more sustainable and easy. I am a very indecisive person and often buy clothes that don't fit the overall aesthetic of my closet. I end up not wearing these clothes and they become a waste, which is why I thought of this feature.

This feature of my project also gives the AI the ability to detect whether or not a piece of clothing that the user wants to buy is similar to what is already in their clothing. After being recommended a piece of a clothing, the user can click on the image of the clothing and be lead to the website that sells it. However, if the AI detects that the user already has something similar in their closet, the user will be prompted with a screen that displays the piece of clothing that is similar to piece they're about to buy.

An upcycling idea generator...

Fashion trends die very quickly and to keep up with the trends, people buy new clothes and leave their old ones dusting in the closet and eventually in landfills. This is known as overconsumerism, where people are encouraged to buy more clothes than they need. To combat this, a lot of people tend to upcycle their old clothing. However, not everyone is experienced in tailoring or fashion design. The Upcycling Chatbot combats this issue by making it easy to come up with ideas on how to reuse clothing.

It can generate ideas on how to upcycle a piece of clothing that a user provides. Instead of leaving your old clothes in your closet and wasting money on new ones, the Upcycling Chatbot makes it easy for you to keep up with fashion trends and save money, as well as save the environment. The AI chatbot would be fed information about the your style as well, so the ideas would also be based on the your preferences. For example, if I swiped "I like it!" on a lot of tank tops and were to ask the chatbot how to upcycle an old T-shirt, the chatbot would generate an idea of how to turn my T-shirt into a tank top. Additionally, the chatbot would be able to generate the materials and steps based on the measurements of the clothing the user provided, and the style that the user prefers.

My Sustainable Closet | Browser Extension Edition

As a browser extension, My Sustainable Closet would make it easy for online shoppers to identify which brands are sustainable, and which are not. While on a website of a fashion brand that is not sustainable, a small popup would appear to notify the user that the brand they're shopping from is harmful to the environment. A small symbol would also appear on the search page next to brands that are not sustainable. For example, in the image below, a small symbol would appear next to the links to H&M and Urban Outfitters.

In addition to this feature, any known information about a brand's sustainability can be accessed through the browser extension. A user would simply have to search the name of the brand, or go to the brand's website, and then click on the browser extension. The user will be provided with the option to access information about the brand, which is similar to the function of the website Good On You.

Additionally, the browser extension lets users add clothes that they just purchased to their virtual closet, rather than adding it manually or through the camera. This makes it easy for users to track how many clothes they've purchased to prevent overconsumerism and a waste of money.
